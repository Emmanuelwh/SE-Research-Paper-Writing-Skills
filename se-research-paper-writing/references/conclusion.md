# Conclusion Writing Guide

## Goal

Write a conclusion that restates the SE problem, summarizes the paper's evidence-backed contribution, and explains implications without introducing new claims.

## Structure

1. Restate the problem and why it matters to SE.
2. Summarize the contribution in one or two concrete sentences.
3. Summarize the strongest evidence, scoped to the study setting.
4. State implications for researchers, practitioners, or tool builders.
5. Mention bounded future work only if it follows from the limitations.

## Rules

1. Do not introduce new results or claims.
2. Do not repeat the abstract mechanically.
3. Keep generality aligned with Threats to Validity.
4. Prefer precise implications over broad promises.

## Template

`This paper addressed [SE problem] in [setting]. We presented/conducted [contribution], showing that [main evidence-backed result]. These results suggest [implication] for [stakeholder], while the scope remains bounded by [main limitation].`
