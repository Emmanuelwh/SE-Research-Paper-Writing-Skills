# Does My SE Writing Flow?

## Quick Flow Test

Use this checklist to diagnose paragraph and section flow in SE papers.

## Paragraph-Level Questions

1. Does the paragraph have exactly one message?
2. Does the first sentence state the message?
3. Are all important nouns explicit: task, stakeholder, artifact, dataset, metric, RQ, or claim?
4. Does each sentence explain, contrast, justify, refine, or provide evidence for the previous sentence?
5. Does the paragraph end by preparing the next paragraph or completing the current role?

## Section-Level Reverse Outline

1. Write the section thesis or RQ.
2. List every paragraph's first sentence.
3. Label each paragraph role: context, problem, gap, method, evidence, implication, limitation.
4. Remove or revise paragraphs that do not support the section thesis.
5. Check whether evidence appears after the claim it supports and before the implication drawn from it.

## Common SE Flow Problems

1. Moving from broad software importance directly to a method without a concrete SE gap.
2. Introducing terms such as dataset, benchmark, tool, model, or framework before defining their role.
3. Switching between task, method, dataset, and claim without transitions.
4. Reporting numbers before explaining the RQ or metric.
5. Hiding limitations until Threats to Validity even though they affect claim wording earlier.
